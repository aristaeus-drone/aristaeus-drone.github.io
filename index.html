The Challenge: A Silent Crisis

Pollination is a vital process that supports our planet's ecosystems and food supply. In fact, 75% of flowering plants and 35% of global crops depend on animal pollinators. However, factors like climate change and pesticide use are causing a dangerous decline in pollinator populations, with nearly 50 species now threatened or endangered. This decline poses a serious risk to global food security and the health of our forests.

Our Solution: Aristaeus

Aristaeus is a complimentary pollination micro-drone system designed to assist, not replace, the natural pollination process. Our goal is to help sustain biodiversity and promote forest regeneration in areas lacking sufficient animal pollinators. By leveraging cutting-edge technology, we can make a targeted impact where it's needed most.

Autonomous Flight & Navigation

The drone navigates complex environments on its own, identifying target trees and flowers without human intervention.

AI-Powered Recognition

Using advanced image processing, Aristaeus accurately identifies specific tree species and locates flowers ready for pollination.

Targeted Pollen Delivery

A non-destructive and effective delivery system ensures pollen is transferred precisely, maximizing pollination success.

Meet the Interdisciplinary Team

Aristaeus is brought to life by a dedicated team of engineering students, combining expertise from multiple fields to tackle this complex challenge.

Mechanical Engineering

Designing and prototyping the drone's structure using CAD modeling, stress analysis, and advanced material selection.

Aerospace Engineering

Optimizing flight efficiency and stability through aerodynamic simulations and rigorous flight testing.

Computer & Electrical Engineering

Developing the brains and nervous system: firmware, flight control algorithms, sensor integration, and power systems.
